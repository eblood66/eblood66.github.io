Harriet Tubman, Secret Agent: How Daring Slaves and Free Blacks Spied for the Union During the Civil War


If it was not amazing enough that Harriet Tubman escaped slavery and then helped hundreds of slaves escape to freedom with the underground railroad, she also spied on the south during the Civil War. Thomas B. Allen tells her tale in Harriet Tubman, Secret Agent: . From his research using numerous original sources, including memoirs of  freed slaves and their enslaved compatriots; he weaves the story of Tubman's and her agents essential role in winning the war. He also writes about the underground railroad,which was another espionage organization. The book offers illustrations, charts, and timelines to help explain this important part of American history. Harriet Tubman's story is a must for anyone interested in real spies.
